Executive Summary

This document maps DialMaster’s data practices to the Google Play Data Safety categories and contains ready-to-publish declarations. Use this as the authoritative reference when completing the Play Console Data Safety form.

Status App collects minimal personal data and shares limited technical data with advertising & analytics partners for operational and monetization purposes.

2. Data Explicitly NOT Collected

For Google Play policy compliance and user transparency, explicitly declare the following as NOT collected (server-side):

  • Contacts: Not uploaded or stored on servers (unless opt‑in backup enabled).
  • Call logs: Not accessed or transmitted.
  • SMS / Messages: Not accessed.
  • Audio recordings & microphone capture: Not recorded by the App.
  • Precise GPS location: Not collected. Only approximate IP‑derived location used for ad auctions or geofencing if required.

3. Shared With Third Parties?

Declared: Yes — We share limited technical and advertising data with advertising, mediation, analytics and crash reporting partners. Use the Play Console field "Shared with third parties" = Yes and list partners.

Purpose of sharing

  • Ad delivery, auctioning (RTB), frequency capping and personalization (with consent).
  • Analytics & crash reporting for product improvement.
  • Fraud detection and reward validation for rewarded ads.

Example partners to list in Play Console (update live list as partners change): AppLovin MAX, Google AdMob, Google Ad Manager (GAM), Prebid Mobile, Unity Ads, ironSource, InMobi, PubMatic, Smaato, Vungle, Playwire, Firebase (Analytics & Crashlytics), RevenueCat (iOS).

4. Encryption & Security (Transit & At Rest)

Declare encryption methods in Play Console:

  • Encryption in Transit: All network traffic uses HTTPS/TLS (TLS 1.2+ recommended) for API calls, SDK transmissions and purchase verification.
  • Encryption at Rest: Sensitive server data stored in Firestore is encrypted at rest by Google Cloud. Local device storage uses platform encryption (Android: encrypted Room / Keystore; iOS: Keychain / Secure Enclave) where available.

Ensure you reference your cloud provider's encryption (Google Cloud Platform) and mention TLS version supported. For Play Console, mark "Encrypted in transit" = Yes and "Encrypted at rest" = Yes.

5. Data Deletion Option

Declare how users can delete data in Play Console. Provide exact instructions.

Server‑side data

To request deletion of server‑side account data (profile, subscriptions, purchase history), users should email: support@dialmaster.in with the subject line Delete My Account. We will verify requests and delete server‑side data within a reasonable timeframe subject to legal retention obligations.

Local data

User local data (contacts, notes, coins) can be deleted via the App's UI or by uninstalling the App. If cloud backup is enabled, the backup deletion procedure will be described during opt‑in.

For Play Console, mark "User can request deletion" = Yes and provide the support email and deletion process details.

6. Validation & SDK Privacy Policies

Play Console requires you to validate SDKs used. For each integrated SDK, confirm:

  • SDK name and version
  • Purpose (ads, analytics, crash reporting)
  • Link to SDK privacy policy
  • Whether the SDK shares data with other third parties

8. Developer Notes & Compliance Tips

  • Test the Data Safety form against the actual network traffic produced by the app; use network logs to verify what SDKs transmit.
  • Keep a live "Advertising Partners We Work With" page on your website and reference it from Play Console.
  • Document retention durations for analytics and rewarded ad logs in your internal SOPs (recommended 90 days for rewarded ad logs).
  • Periodically audit SDK versions and remove unused/legacy SDKs that may collect unexpected data.

Compliance reminder: Google may reject or flag your app if the Data Safety form is inaccurate. Be conservative — declare all categories SDKs might transmit even if you don't directly use the data.
